Chowmein Recipe


Home Page

Description

Chowmein is a popular stir-fried noodle dish in Nepal, India, and China. It’s made by tossing boiled noodles with vegetables, meat, and sauces in a hot wok, giving it a smoky flavor. Street-style chowmein is usually spicy and tangy, while homemade versions can be mild and customizable.

Ingredients

  • Noodles – the base, boiled until just soft.
  • Vegetables – cabbage, carrots, capsicum, onions, and spring onions (sliced thin).
  • Meat (optional) – chicken, pork, or buffalo (cut into thin strips).
  • Garlic & ginger – finely chopped for flavor.
  • Green chilies – for heat.
  • Soy sauce – adds salty umami taste.
  • Tomato ketchup / chili sauce – for tang and spice.
  • Vinegar – a splash for sharpness.
  • Oil – for stir-frying at high heat.
  • Salt & pepper – to taste.

Steps

  1. Boil noodles – Cook until just done, drain, and toss with a little oil to prevent sticking.
  2. Prepare vegetables & meat – Slice everything thin for quick cooking.
  3. Stir-fry meat – In a hot wok, fry the meat strips until cooked; set aside.
  4. Cook vegetables – Add oil, then garlic, ginger, onions, and remaining vegetables. Stir-fry on high heat.
  5. Combine – Add noodles, meat, soy sauce, vinegar, ketchup, chili sauce, salt, and pepper. Toss everything quickly so it mixes well without turning soggy.
  6. Garnish & serve – Top with chopped spring onions and serve hot.
